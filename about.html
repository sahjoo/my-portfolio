My Story



👶🏻 Growing up, I loved classic Disney movies and whimsical books. I started sketching Disney characters and animals, and my interest in art was born. Years later during the era of dial-up internet, I opened a Xanga account and spent many hours designing my Xanga page (R.I.P Xanga). I had so much fun learning coding and making my page as tacky as possible!

👩🏻‍🎓 For my undergrad, I majored in graphic design, but I was afraid of failure. I found a fascination in the human mind and body so I thought, why not become a doctor? That's safe. Goodbye studio, hello lab! Unfortunately along the way, someone dear to me became sick and I put my career on hold to become a caretaker. I learned that I hated seeing someone in pain and didn't think I could cut it as a doctor. My friend informed me I'm too much of an empath, and she was so right.

💲 Needing to work, I found an opportunity at a debt collection management company. I soon realized that this industry wasn't right for me either because once again, it was sad. But during my time there, I learned so much about myself and finally figured out what I wanted. Being able to collaborate with my coworkers and solve problems were what motivated me. I enjoyed designing event emails and banners that made people smile and encouraged morale. And I learned I could do all this as a user experience designer! How perfect! I was ready to make a change.

👩🏻‍💻 To jumpstart my design life, I enrolled into an immersive design program where I learned visual design, interaction design, experience design and front-end development. I worked with senior-level designers on projects from the start of the design process to final prototypes or development. Now as a designer, I have learned to welcome failure as it helps me grow. When I was a caretaker and employee in the debt collection industry, I saw problems that I wished I could solve. I know now I can't do it alone. But by working together using human-centered design, I know we can solve these problems.

🛩 In my spare time, I love exploring new cafes, eats and breweries, reading novels or comics, listening to podcasts. Most of all, I love spending time with friends and family.
